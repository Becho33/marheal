What happens in a session

  1. The client typically lies down on a comfortable massage table, fully clothed.

  2. The practitioner begins by placing their hands on or near the client's body, in various positions that correspond to the body's energy centers, or chakras.

  3. The practitioner channels Reiki energy into the client's body, using gentle touch or hovering their hands just above the body.

  4. The client may feel warmth, tingling, or other sensations during the treatment, as the Reiki energy begins to work on balancing and harmonizing the body's energy.

  5. The practitioner may use symbols or other techniques to help direct the flow of energy and address specific areas of imbalance or tension in the body.

  6. The treatment typically lasts anywhere from 60 to 90 minutes, depending on the client's needs and preferences.

  7. After the treatment, the client may feel deeply relaxed, rejuvenated, and refreshed, with a greater sense of overall well-being.

Science and Reiki

Science is beginning to catch up to what Reiki practitioners have known for decades - that this healing practice is powerful and effective. Recent studies have shown that Reiki can have a positive impact on a wide range of physical and emotional ailments, from reducing stress and anxiety to improving immune function and reducing pain.


One study conducted by the National Institutes of Health found that Reiki was effective in reducing pain and anxiety in patients undergoing knee replacement surgery. Another study found that Reiki improved immune function in breast cancer patients undergoing radiation therapy. These are just a few examples of the growing body of research supporting the use of Reiki in healthcare settings.


If you're interested in learning more about the scientific evidence behind Reiki, there are many resources available online. The International Center for Reiki Training maintains a comprehensive database of Reiki research studies, which you can access at https://www.researchgate.net/search?q=reiki. The Center for Reiki Research also has a wealth of information on the scientific studies and clinical trials that have been conducted on Reiki, which you can find at https://www.centerforreikiresearch.org/.


Whether you're a healthcare professional looking to integrate Reiki into your practice, or simply someone interested in the healing arts, exploring the scientific evidence behind Reiki can deepen your understanding of this powerful healing practice.
